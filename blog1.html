Data Visualisation

Caylon Smith
5 min
12 August 2022

What is that

The quantitative data and information are abstract as they describe things that are not physical. As a result of quantitative data and information being abstract, they become confusing and unusable therefore we must devise ways to present the quantitative data and information more physically and qualitatively. (Fullstory, 2022)

We can represent quantitative data and information more physically and qualitatively by using Data visualization (Fullstory, 2022). Data visualization is the representation of quantitative data and information in a graphical context using pictorial elements such as Tables, charts, graphs, trees, scatter plots, diagrams, and maps (brush & Burns, 2020) (Li, 2020). Data visualization aids human cognition and provides new insights while also teaching the reader about said information. (Hazzard, 2015) (Li, 2020)

What exactly though are we trying to reinterpret in a visual context? what exactly is quantitative data and information?

Data is raw unprocessed information and usually has less to no meaning. Data is represented as individual quantitative figures and numbers (Li, 2020). Information on the other hand is data that has been processed it’s the perception of the data that has been collected. Information is most often used and has meaning attached. (Hill, 2021)

Example scenario: the English premiere league is a football association and league (premier league, 2022) in which teams compete against each other to get more points than any other team and therefore win the league. As a result, they must collect data such as top goal scorers in the league the collection of the people who scored the goals in the league alone is classified as data as those statistics on their own mean nothing but through the processing of that information I could determine patterns like which clubs have more goal scorers than the others, for example, the top goal scorers for the league last year had jota, mane, and salah, they all play for Liverpool and Liverpool finished close second it would not be a stretch to say that Liverpool having those players score those many goals were a factor in Liverpool finishing so high. The process is simple: collect and interpret data, gain information, present information, and gain insight. (premier league, 2022)

Now, what is quantitative data? Well, this is collected data that have a numerical value, this data is measured in numbers and counted in them too. Quantitative data answers questions such as how many and how much. (Fullstory, 2022)

We can collect two types of data being, secondary data, and primary data. Secondary data is data that has already been collected you can use this data to help guide your research or make your data feel a more concrete example of secondary data might be a company database that contains information collected previously by the company in the past. Primary Data is data that you collect and can manipulate variables around the collection of the data. ( Formplus Blog, 2022)

Cognition is primarily managed by the cerebral cortex, to process data and or information on its own takes a lot of effort and thinking, the process of this data is also quite slow and thus hard (Few, 2022) (Mnguni, 2014). Data visualization presents information in a way that our eyes can judge and understand, it lessens the processing load of our brain as it uses its abilities more efficiently (Few, 2022). Data visualization uses human visual perception to process information extremely fast and efficiently, we immediately require less cognition (i.e thinking) and therefore understand and interpret the information intuitively. Visual perception is controlled by the visual cortex. (Few, 2022) (Li, 2020) (Mnguni, 2014)

To achieve this intuitive characteristic, our graphical representation has to be efficient accurate, and meaningful. Some guiding questions to ask may be as follows (Few, 2022):

  • Does my representation clearly indicate the nature of the relationship between the values?
  • Does my representation represent the quantities accurately?
  • Does my representation make it easy to compare the quantities?
  • Does my representation make it easy to see the ranked order of values?
  • Does my representation make it obvious how people should use the information?

Data visualization in games

Now to understand Data visualization even further let’s look at how they function in games. Most if not all games at their core are about learning whether it be learning the game’s main mechanics or rules, most of the time we will be learning and using what we learned to beat or play the game. Data visualization provides a way to help a player learn and understand the game space in which they are participating. (Hazzard, 2015) (Meeks, 2018)

Game data visualization types:

Non-diegetic data visualizations

Many games utilize data visualizations that are non-diegetic. These visualizations are generally made to not look like they belong or exist in the video games world. These visualizations exist to only communicate information to the player (Hazzard, 2015) (Meeks, 2018)

diegetic data visualizations

Some games use utilize data visualizations that are diegetic. These visualizations are generally made to fit inside the video games world. These visualizations exist to assist the players moment to moment gameplay decisions, for example, some games color code enemies by the difficulty which therefore could help the player decide if they are equipped to engage with that enemy or not. data visualizations put into a diegetic form help make a game feel more immersive and make the visualizations less just pasted on. (Hazzard, 2015) (Meeks, 2018)

Why have data visualization In games though?

Data visualization helps the brain think less as a result because the player is thinking less they are not only able to learn the rules of the game easier and faster they can have fun and not quit the game. As a result of needing to think, fewer players can immerse themselves more into the game and enter a flow state. Overall Data visualization helps communicate vital information to players and helps in crafting an amazing user experience.

In conclusion, Data visualization is a key element in modern human information processing, it can be used in different facets of life, business, and sectors not covered here. The look at data visualization in games is just but a small piece of evidence as to how powerful of a tool it is and how advantageous it could be to learn it.
